    <h2>Entrees</h2>
    <article>
        <h3>Penne alla Christi</h3>
        <img src="http://media-cache-ec0.pinimg.com/736x/1e/89/be/1e89bedbb7713eda17c2fbe9b868559a.jpg" alt="Delicious bowl of Penne alla Christi" title="Penne with a creamy tomato sauce" height="200px">
            <h4>Ingredients</h4>
                <ul>
                    <li>12 Chicken Tenderloins</li>
                    <li>2 T olive oil</li>
                    <li>2 T diced garlic (more if you like garlic)</li>
                    <li>1/2 cup sliced sun-dried tomatoes in oil</li>
                    <li>2 jars marinara sauce</li>
                    <li>2 cups heavy cream</li>
                    <li>1/2 c grated parmesan or parmesan romano blend cheese</li>
                    <li>1 bag baby spinach (washed)</li>
                </ul>
            <h4>Preparation</h4>
                <ol>
                    <li> Cook chicken tenderloins until just cooked</li>
                    <li>Remove from pan and cut into bite sized pieces </li>
                    <li> Put 2 T olive oil into 8 qt sauce pan</li>
                    <li>Saute garlic and sundried tomatos </li>
                    <li> Add 2 cups heavy cream and cook on medium until just begins to bubble</li>
                    <li>Add 2 jars marinara sauce (I use Ragu or Bertolli)</li>
                    <li>Cook over medium heat until reduced stirring every couple minutes</li>
                    <li>Stir in 1/2 c grated parmesan cheese</li>
                    <li>Stir in spinach until wilted</li>
                    <li>Stir in cooked chicken</li>
                    <li>Cover and cook on low until pasta is ready</li>
                    <li>Cook 1 lb. Penne or your favorite pasta ( I use small bowtie pasta)</li>
                    <li>Drain pasta </li>
                </ol>
        <h4>Serve sauce over a bowl full of pasta. Great with italian bread for dipping in sauce. <br>Serves 6</h4>
    </article>
</article>
<article id="sides">
    <h2>Sides</h2>
    <article>
        <h3>Scrumptious Cheddar and Bacon Twice Baked Potatoes</h3>
        <img src="http://www.instructables.com/files/deriv/FK1/YL3N/G23UX5J2/FK1YL3NG23UX5J2.LARGE.jpg" alt="Twice baked potatoes topped with cheese and bacon" title="Scrumptious Cheddar and Bacon Twice Baked Potatos" height="200px">
            <h4>Ingredients</h4>
                <ul>
                    <li>4 large pototoes</li>
                    <li>1 3oz. bag bacon bits</li>
                    <li>1 8oz. bag sharp shredded cheese</li>
                    <li>butter</li>
                </ul>
        <h4>Preparation</h4>
            <ol>
                <li>Preheat oven to 350 degrees</li>
                <li>Wash potatoes, dry, pierce on top with knife, wrap in paper towel</li>
                <li>Microwave 5 mins, turn over</li>
                <li>Microwave till done, they will be squishy (about 5-7 mins more depending on the size of the potato) </li>
                <li>Cut a slice of the skin off the top of each potato</li>
                <li>Hallow out potatoes placing potato pulp in large mixing bowl leaving the skins intact</li>
                <li>Place skins on baking sheet</li>
                <li>Mash the potatoes in the bowl with a fork</li>
                <li>Mix in 1/2 cup softened butter and 1 cup cheese and stir till mixed well </li>
                <li>Place potato mixture inside the skins</li>
                <li>Top each potato with remaining cheese and bacon bits</li>
                <li>Place in oven and bake for 10 mins</li>
             </ol>
     </article>
</article>

<article id="desserts">
    <h2>Desserts</h2>
    <img src="http://media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/d1/35/13/d135134cdd1aa2670b23852fbf262a43.jpg" alt="Slice of Two Layer Brownie Cake" title="Decadent Two Layer Brownie Cake" height="200px" >
    <article>
        <h3>Birthday Brownie Cake</h3>
            <h4>Ingredients</h4>
                <ul>
                    <li>2 boxes Ghirardelli Double Chocolate Brownies</li>
                    <li>eggs</li>
                    <li>canola oil</li>
                    <li>1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips (divided)</li>
                    <li>1 tub Pillsbury Creamy Supreme Chocolate Fudge Frosting</li>
                    <li>Pam baking spray with flour (the blue can)(not cooking spray)</li>
                    <li>2 9x9 glass baking pans</li>
                </ul>
        <h4>Preparation</h4>
            <ol>
                <li>Preheat oven to temperature listed on box for glass pans</li>
                <li>Spray bottom only of pans with Pam baking spray</li>
                <li>Make brownies as directed on box with the addition of 1/2 choc. chips for each</li>
                <li>Cook according to package directions for the minimum time listed</li>
                <li>When brownies are cool at least 20 minutes - ice one pan of brownies with 1/2 of the icing</li>
                <li>Loosen brownies in other pan and remove from pan and place on top of iced brownies</li>
                <li>Ice brownie cake with the rest of the icing</li>
            </ol>
        <h4>Delicious served with chocolate or vanilla ice cream</h4>
     </article>
